What Happens During a Cash Home Sale? A Step-by-Step Guide

Curious what actually happens when you sell for cash? Here's the honest, step-by-step process from first call to closing day.

If you've never sold a home to a cash buyer before, the process can feel unfamiliar. What actually happens? How fast does it move? What do you need to do? This post walks you through the entire process so you know exactly what to expect when you work with S+S Home Solutions.

Step One: You Reach Out

Everything starts with a simple conversation. You contact us, share some basic information about your property, and we set up a time to learn more about your situation. There's no pressure and no commitment at this stage — just a conversation.

Step Two: We Learn About Your Property

We'll ask some questions about the property's condition, your timeline, and what you're hoping to accomplish. In some cases we'll schedule a brief walkthrough of the property. This helps us put together an offer that is fair and realistic.

Step Three: You Receive Your Offer

Based on what we learn, we put together a written cash offer. This typically happens within a few days. The offer is straightforward — no confusing contingencies, no complicated terms. Just a clear number and a proposed closing timeline.

Step Four: You Decide

You take as much time as you need to review the offer. We encourage you to ask questions, compare your options, and make the decision that is right for your situation. There is never any pressure to accept.

Step Five: We Open Escrow and Begin the Closing Process

If you accept, we move into closing. Because there's no lender involved, this process moves much faster than a traditional sale. A title company handles the paperwork and confirms that the title is clean and transferable.

Step Six: You Close and Get Paid

On the closing date, you sign the documents, the title transfers, and you receive your funds — typically via wire transfer. In many cases this entire process from first conversation to closing takes two to four weeks.

That's it. Simple, clean, and certain.
